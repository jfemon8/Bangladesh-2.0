Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh has the potential for innovation, but its performance is lower than many other countries. However, there have been many innovations and initiatives in recent years.

Sustainability

Bangladesh is highly vulnerable to climate change and environmental degradation. Bangladesh is one of the most vulnerable countries to climate change.

Infrastructure

With Bangladesh on track to graduate from the UN's Least Developed Countries list in 2026, new infrastructure is helping open up the country's domestic and international trade.

Empowerment

Bangladesh has made significant progress in empowering women and girls in many areas, including education, employment, and political representation.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Bangladesh Launches New Satellite

Date: 05/01/2025

Time: 10.25 PM

The Bangabandhu Satellite-1 (BS-1) is the first geostationary communications satellite of Bangladesh, launched on May 11, 2018, from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida, USA, by SpaceX using the Falcon 9 rocket. The satellite covers the entire Bangladesh region, as well as SAARC countries, parts of Southeast Asia, and Africa. It helps reduce reliance on foreign satellites, saving Bangladesh millions of dollars annually.
